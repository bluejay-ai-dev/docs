## Anatomy of a great Digital Human

A Digital Human has three key components:

1. **Persona** — demographics, personality, communication style
2. **Scenario script** — what the caller wants to accomplish, constraints, and fallback behavior
3. **Customer Traits** — structured attributes (e.g., account type, language preference) that add realism

<Tip>
Write scenario scripts in second person ("You are calling to reschedule an appointment…") to give the Digital Human clear agency.
</Tip>

## Organizing with Communities

When you have a library of Digital Humans, group them into **Communities**. A Community lets you:

- Attach a batch of personas to a Simulation with one click
- Keep test coverage consistent across simulation runs
- Share personas across teams

For the full reference, see [Key Concepts → Communities](/key-concepts/communities/overview).

## Knowledge check

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What makes a scenario script effective?">
    A good scenario script is specific about the caller's goal, includes constraints (budget, time), and describes fallback behavior if the agent can't help.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="When should you use Communities vs. inline Digital Humans?">
    Use Communities when the same set of personas should be reused across multiple simulations. Use inline Digital Humans for one-off, scenario-specific personas.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
