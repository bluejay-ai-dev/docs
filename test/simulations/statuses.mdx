Every simulated call in Bluejay reports a status. So does every simulation run that groups those calls together. This page lists each one: what it means, why a call might land there, and what to do about it.

## Two layers of status

Bluejay tracks status at two levels.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Simulation Result" icon="phone">
    One row per **simulated call**. Where the call is in its lifecycle, and if it failed, why.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Simulation Run" icon="layer-group">
    One row per **group of calls** launched together. Whether the group is still progressing or has finished. The pass/fail/cancel mix appears next to the status.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Simulation Result statuses

A simulation result is one simulated call. Its status moves through several **in‑progress** states before settling on exactly one **terminal** state.

### In progress

These appear for a few seconds to a couple of minutes during a normal run. No action needed; they progress automatically.

| Status | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Initializing** | The call has been created and is being prepared. |
| **Queued** | Waiting for a worker to pick it up. |
| **Ready** | For inbound digital humans (ones that receive calls). The digital human is online and waiting for your agent to call in. |
| **Dispatched** | The worker is starting the call. The phone hasn't connected yet. |
| **Running** | The call is connected and the conversation is happening. |
| **Evaluation Pending** | We've queued your call for evaluation. Scoring will happen momentarily. |
| **Evaluating** | The transcript is being scored against your success criteria. |

### Completed

| Status | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Completed** | The call ran end‑to‑end and was scored. Pass or fail against your success criteria appears separately. |

### Failure buckets

When a call doesn't reach **Completed**, it lands in one of six failure buckets. Each one tells you *who* needs to do something about it.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="No Connection: couldn't reach your agent" icon="plug-circle-xmark">
    **What happened:** We couldn't reach your agent at all. Common causes: bad credentials for your provider, a wrong agent ID, an unverified caller ID, an unreachable SIP destination, or your provider's concurrency limit was hit before the call could connect.

    **Who needs to fix it:** Usually your config. Sometimes your provider's plan limits.

    **What to do:** Click the row in the simulation results table to see the specific error code and message.

    - If it's about credentials or agent IDs, check your integration settings in **Settings → Integrations**.
    - If it's about concurrency, raise the limit on your provider account or stagger your runs so fewer calls go out at once.
    - If it's about caller ID or SIP routing, confirm the number or destination is verified on your side.

    **Error codes you might see:**

    | Group | Codes | What they mean |
    |---|---|---|
    | Provider authentication | `ELEVEN_LABS_AUTH_FAILED`, `RETELL_AUTH_FAILED`, `VAPI_AUTH_FAILED`, `WEBSOCKET_AUTH_FAILED` | Your provider rejected the API key Bluejay has on file. |
    | Missing integration | `MISSING_ELEVEN_LABS_INTEGRATION`, `MISSING_RETELL_INTEGRATION`, `MISSING_VAPI_INTEGRATION`, `MISSING_PIPECAT_INTEGRATION`, `NO_PIPECAT_AGENT_NAME` | The provider integration isn't connected to your organization, or isn't fully configured. |
    | Missing agent ID | `MISSING_ELEVEN_LABS_AGENT_ID`, `MISSING_RETELL_AGENT_ID`, `MISSING_VAPI_ASSISTANT_ID` | This digital human's agent or assistant ID is empty. |
    | Credential decryption | `ELEVEN_LABS_INTEGRATION_DECRYPT_FAILED`, `RETELL_INTEGRATION_DECRYPT_FAILED`, `VAPI_INTEGRATION_DECRYPT_FAILED` | We have credentials on file but couldn't decrypt them. Usually fixed by reconnecting the integration. |
    | Bridge connect | `ELEVEN_LABS_BRIDGE_CONNECT_FAILED`, `RETELL_BRIDGE_CONNECT_FAILED`, `VAPI_BRIDGE_CONNECT_FAILED`, `ELEVEN_LABS_SESSION_SETUP_FAILED`, `RETELL_SESSION_SETUP_FAILED`, `VAPI_SESSION_SETUP_FAILED` | The provider accepted the connection request but failed to start a session. Often a transient provider issue. |
    | Telephony / SIP | `UNUSABLE_CALLER_ID`, `INVALID_PHONE_NUMBER`, `INVALID_SIP_DESTINATION`, `SIP_DIAL_FAILED` | The outbound call couldn't be placed because the caller ID isn't verified, the destination is malformed, or the SIP dial itself failed. |
    | Concurrency and pre-start | `CUSTOMER_CONCURRENCY_LIMIT`, `BRIDGE_PRE_START_FAILED`, `NO_CONNECTION_TYPE` | Your provider's concurrent-call limit was hit, pre-flight bridge setup failed, or the digital human has no configured connection type. |
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="No Answer: reached the line but nobody engaged" icon="phone-slash">
    **What happened:** We reached your phone number or SIP endpoint, but nobody engaged. Either nobody picked up, the call was rejected, or the agent hung up before speaking.

    **Who needs to fix it:** Customer side (your agent or your line).

    **What to do:** Confirm that:

    - Your agent is configured to accept calls from Bluejay's caller ID.
    - Your agent is online.
    - Your phone or SIP routing is healthy and routes Bluejay traffic correctly.

    **Error codes you might see:**

    | Code | What it means |
    |---|---|
    | `NO_ANSWER` | The phone rang out without anyone picking up. |
    | `CALL_REJECTED` | Your agent received the call and explicitly rejected it (SIP 486 or 603). |
    | `AGENT_HUNG_UP_BEFORE_SPEECH` | Your agent picked up but hung up before speaking. |
    | `INBOUND_NO_INCOMING_CALL` | For inbound digital humans: the digital human waited for a call from your agent but never received one. |
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Call Dropped: call started, then died mid-conversation" icon="phone-arrow-down-left">
    **What happened:** The call connected and a real conversation started, but the line dropped mid‑call. Most often a provider‑side issue on your agent.

    **Who needs to fix it:** Customer side (your agent or your provider).

    **What to do:** The transcript up to the drop point is still available and gets scored normally, so you don't lose data from before the drop. If you see a cluster of dropped calls in a single run:

    - Check your voice provider's status page.
    - Review any recent agent config changes that may have introduced instability.

    **Error codes you might see:**

    | Group | Codes | What they mean |
    |---|---|---|
    | Provider agent error | `ELEVEN_LABS_AGENT_ERROR`, `RETELL_AGENT_ERROR`, `VAPI_AGENT_ERROR` | Your provider's voice agent reported an error mid-call. |
    | Provider agent start failure | `ELEVEN_LABS_AGENT_START_FAILED`, `RETELL_AGENT_START_FAILED`, `VAPI_AGENT_START_FAILED` | Your provider's agent failed to start its turn during the call. |
    | Provider monitoring error | `ELEVEN_LABS_MONITORING_ERROR`, `RETELL_MONITORING_ERROR`, `VAPI_MONITORING_ERROR` | Bluejay lost the monitoring connection to your provider mid-call. |
    | Bridge disconnect | `PIPECAT_CONNECTION_FAILED`, `BRIDGE_DISCONNECTED` | The audio bridge between Bluejay and your provider went down. |
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Agent Rejection: your agent hit a system failure, so the call couldn't be evaluated" icon="ban">
    **What happened:** Your agent did speak, but its output was a system‑ or infrastructure‑level failure on your side — phrases like "not enough funds," "max concurrency reached," "servers overloaded," "out of memory," or "internal error" — instead of actually handling the scenario. A system error took the place of real work, so there was no meaningful conversation to score. This can show up at the very start of the call or after the agent breaks part‑way through.

    **Who needs to fix it:** Customer side (your agent or its upstream limits).

    **What to do:** Click the row to see the exact failure message your agent returned. Then:

    - If it's a capacity or concurrency message, raise the limit on your provider or stagger your runs so fewer calls go out at once.
    - If it's a billing/funds message, top up the balance on your own provider account (this is your agent's balance, not your Bluejay balance).
    - If it's a generic "servers overloaded / try again later," check your provider's status and any recent deploys.

    To retry these automatically, enable **Rerun Simulation Results On An Error** and its **Include Rerunning On Agent Rejection** option in **Settings → Simulations**; matching results then rerun up to 3 times.

    **Error codes you might see:**

    | Code | What it means |
    |---|---|
    | `AGENT_REJECTED` | Your agent returned a system/infrastructure failure message (out of resources, out of funds, internal error, etc.) instead of handling the call, leaving no real content to evaluate. |
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Out of Funds: Bluejay balance exhausted" icon="wallet">
    **What happened:** Your Bluejay balance ran out before the call could complete.

    **Who needs to fix it:** Bluejay account admin.

    **What to do:** Top up your balance in **Settings → Billing**, then re‑run the affected calls.

    **Error codes you might see:**

    | Code | What it means |
    |---|---|
    | `INSUFFICIENT_BALANCE` | Your Bluejay account balance reached zero before the call could complete. |
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="System Error: something inside Bluejay broke" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    **What happened:** Something inside Bluejay broke during your call. The evaluation pipeline, recording upload, or an internal service may have failed.

    **Who needs to fix it:** Bluejay.

    **What to do:** Reach out to the Bluejay team to learn more about why your call failed. We log every failure and often have a fix in hand before customers reach out, so getting in touch is the fastest way to find out where things stand.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Cancelled

| Status | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Cancelled** | You stopped the call before it started running. Only available while a call is still in the early stages (Initializing, Queued, Ready, or Dispatched). Once a call is actually running, you can't cancel it; it will either complete or drop. |

## Simulation Run statuses

A simulation run is the *group* of simulated calls you launched together. Its status is simpler than the per‑call status: a run is either still happening or it has finished.

| Status | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Running** | At least one call in the run is still in flight (queued, dispatched, running, or being evaluated). |
| **Completed** | Every call in the run has reached an end state, whether it passed, failed, or was cancelled. |

The pass/fail/cancel breakdown appears alongside the status. A finished run might display:

> **Completed** · 47 passed · 2 failed · 1 cancelled (out of 50)

<Note>
A simulation run is **Completed** as soon as there's nothing left to wait on. That includes runs where every call failed or every call was cancelled. The mix of outcomes lives in the counters next to the status, which keeps "is the run done?" a clean yes/no question.
</Note>

## Quick reference

| If you see lots of... | Most likely cause | First thing to check |
|---|---|---|
| **No Connection** | Misconfigured integration or hitting a provider concurrency limit | Your provider's API key, agent ID, and current concurrency setting |
| **No Answer** | Your agent isn't picking up | Your agent's phone routing, allowed‑caller‑ID list, online status |
| **Call Dropped** | Your provider is unstable for a window | Your provider's status page; any recent agent config changes |
| **Agent Rejection** | Your agent returns a system failure (capacity, funds, overload, internal error) instead of running the call | Your provider's concurrency/funds limits; the exact failure message on the row |
| **Out of Funds** | Bluejay account balance ran out | Your Bluejay billing settings |
| **System Error** | A Bluejay‑side problem | Reach out to the Bluejay team. We often have a fix in hand before customers ask |

<Tip>
For any specific failure, click the row in the simulation results table to see the exact error code, a detailed message, and a one‑click action where one is available.
</Tip>
